# Research Context
This file provides broader context for the 10 core experts identified in `sources.md`. These supplementary inputs were used to validate the strategic frameworks (CLG), verify conversion tactics, and pressure-test my performance marketing assumptions.

### 1. The Community-Led Growth (CLG) Reality Check
* **Source:** *Community-Led Growth: Building a Pipeline Engine Around Your SaaS Community* (Yury Larichev)
* **Relevance:** This document was critical in moving my research from "theoretical engagement" to "revenue architecture." 
* **My Takeaway:** Larichev’s framework effectively reframes community from a "cost center" to a "pipeline engine." [cite_start]It taught me that successful B2B SaaS community programs aren't about platform features (Slack/Discord), but about the flywheel of certifications, partner programs, and referral loops[cite: 3, 4]. It serves as a necessary counterbalance to "vanity engagement" metrics.

### 2. The Conversion Intent Architecture
* **Source:** *Your pricing page is not a formality* (Vivek Nair)
* **Relevance:** Used to refine the "applied strategy" section of my growth architecture.
* **My Takeaway:** Nair’s teardown highlights that the pricing page is the "moment of maximum intent." It challenged my research by proving that the homepage is often a distraction, whereas the pricing page is the actual conversion bottleneck. [cite_start]This was the primary driver for Phase 2 of my `growth-architecture.md` plan[cite: 9].

### 3. Systems vs. Luck in Performance Marketing
* **Source:** *Performance Marketing Case Studies 2026* (Dibbyyan Nath)
* **Relevance:** Used to validate that performance marketing at scale requires "systems" over "individual hustle."
* **My Takeaway:** Nath provides the hard data to back up my skepticism toward generic marketing advice. [cite_start]The key pattern identified was that "vertical-specific" case studies convert 3.4x better than generic ones[cite: 7]. This data point directly informed my recommendation to move 100Hires toward vertical-specific proof in `growth-architecture.md`.

### Why this matters for the synthesis
These sources helped me avoid the "scale-stage bias." Most growth content assumes a massive company; these sources, particularly Larichev's work on early-stage pipeline, helped me keep my recommendations grounded in the reality of a company aiming to scale rather than an enterprise already at the top.
